Information

General

All concerts are FREE and open to the public. Performances begin at 6:30PM in the Jay Pritzker Pavillion and typically end before 9:00PM.

What to bring

Food, drink, and lawn chairs are allowed in the lawn area. Alcohol is permitted if you're old enough.

Bike parking

Bicycle parking is located just south of Randolph Street on the north end of the park, just east of the pavillion; limited additional bicycle parking is located near the southwest corner of the pavillion lawn.

About this guide

I am not affilliated with Millinnium Park or the City of Chicago.

This is an unofficial, mobile-friendly guide to Millennium Park's wonderful summer concert series. I'm simply a music lover, an enthusiastic attendee, and a Chicagoan.

Every year, I find myself slogging through the City of Chicago's website to find out which artists are performing on which dates. So, this year I decided to make that information easier to find. Hopefully it proves useful to you as well. I'll do my best to keep everything up to date throughout the summer.

Show details and biographical information used on this website are publicly available on the City of Chicago's website.
